Complaints lodged about representatives

Under the regulatory framework, many paid representatives are required to hold a licence to carry out certain activities. This licensing requirement also means they must be AFCA members. As a result, if a consumer lodges a complaint about a paid representative, AFCA may consider and handle it as a complaint against an AFCA member.

Key complaint and industry trends

AFCA may deal with a paid representative in two ways:

  • as a representative – when they lodge complaints
  • as an AFCA member – if a consumer lodges a complaint against them.

Significant increase in complaints lodged by paid representatives

In 2024‑25, 9% of our total complaint volume came from paid representatives lodging on behalf of consumers. At 9,429 complaints, this was an increase of 93% from 2023‑24. It is the highest volume of complaints ever lodged by paid representatives, continuing a year‑on‑year increasing trend.

Surge in add‑on insurance complaints

The rise in complaints by paid representatives was driven largely by add‑on insurance. Complaints increased from 2,258 in 2023‑24 to 7,188 in 2024‑25. Other top complaint products included personal loans (455), credit cards (376), home building insurance (362) and home loans (136).

The most common issue was misleading product or service information at 6,981 complaints. Up from 1,311 in 2023‑24, which reflects add‑on insurance concerns.

Complaints lodged against paid representatives as members

In 2024‑25, we also saw a small, but increasing volume of complaints lodged against paid representatives as AFCA members. Key complaint issues were delays – including delayed communication, resolution and lodgement, and whether fees were clearly explained or appropriately charged.

Resolution and timeframes

Increase in matters reaching Decision

Most complaints were closed at either the Referral stage (2,852) or Case Management stage (2,535). However, 655 complaints reached Decision stage. This reflects both the increased volume of complaints and entrenched positions held by the parties, which meant a final view was needed from us. Complaints closed at Rules Review stage fell to 473 from 1,109 in 2023‑24. Time taken to resolve complaints varied, with 1,864 closed in 30 days and around 50% in less than 180.

Industry trends and challenges

The volume of add‑on insurance complaints lodged by paid representatives created challenges for industry and AFCA. When complaints are lodged in large batches, it can impact a financial firm’s ability to meet timeframes for internal dispute resolution (IDR). In turn, we have to accept and investigate a lot more complaints requiring investigation. This emphasises the importance of financial firms being adequately resourced in IDR and ensuring remediation programs address consumer harm.
