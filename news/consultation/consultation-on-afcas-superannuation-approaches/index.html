Consultation on AFCA’s Superannuation Approaches

Update: AFCA has published three updated superannuation Approaches following extensive consultations with industry, regulators and consumer advocates.

Consultations closed

Update: AFCA has published three updated superannuation Approaches following extensive consultations with industry, regulators and consumer advocates.

Delivered as part of our FY24-25 Approaches Consultation schedule, these new and amended documents clarify AFCA’s approach on a range of complex matters, including:

  • Formal submissions
  • Delayed insurance claims in superannuation(opens in new tab)
  • Sections 29(6) and (7) of the Insurance Contracts Act
  • Superannuation death benefits

About the consultations

Our Approaches are developed and updated to provide greater clarity around what to expect from AFCA processes, greater transparency about the way we investigate complaints and make decisions, and ensure we continue to deliver consistent outcomes for complaints to AFCA.

The documents will help complainants, financial firms and stakeholders understand how AFCA considers complaints in the two key areas of relation to delayed insurance claims in superannuation, sections 29(6) and (7) of the Insurance Contracts Act and superannuation death benefits.

They outline how AFCA applies legal principles, industry codes, regulatory guidance and considers good industry practice when investigating these complaints. The documents are informed by feedback received during our complaint handling process with our stakeholders, including government, regulators, financial firms and consumer advocates.

Together, our updated Approaches are designed to help trustees, members, and their advocates understand what to expect from AFCA’s process. They support faster, more consistent outcomes and reinforce AFCA’s commitment to a free, fair, and independent external dispute resolution scheme.

Consultation feedback report on AFCA’s updated Approach to Superannuation death benefits

AFCA’s updated approach to Superannuation death benefits includes important guidance for trustees in cases involving family violence. This guidance recognises strong community concerns about the risk of superannuation entitlements going to perpetrators of abuse and the profound harm this can entail.

In response to valuable stakeholder feedback, we have updated our Approach document to:

  • The AFCA Approach to Superannuation Death Benefit Complaints(opens in new tab)
  • Read the consultation report here(opens in new tab)
  • Emphasise that trustees must consider each death benefit claim on its own facts to ensure fair and reasonable outcomes.
  • Clarify that the primary purpose is to provide for dependants who would have relied on the member for financial support.
  • Include information on the steps trustees should take once notified of a member's death.
  • Expand information on the scope and implications of binding and non-lapsing nominations.
  • Add further details on assigning priority in death benefit allocation and considerations for financial dependency.
  • Clarify that financial dependency is assessed at the date of death and not affected by future contingent events.
  • Address circumstances where binding nominations cease to be effective.
  • Clarify when a trustee other than the surviving parent should hold a share of the death benefit for a minor child.
  • Include amendments and added more case studies to cover a broader range of scenarios, including those relevant to circumstances of family violence.

Consultation feedback report on AFCA’s new Approaches to delayed insurance claims in superannuation and Sections 29(6) and (7) of the Insurance Contracts Act 1984

In response to valuable stakeholder feedback, we have updated AFCA's Approaches to:

Sections 29(6) and 29(7) of the Insurance Contracts Act 1984:

  • The AFCA Approach to sections 29(6) and 29(7) of the Insurance Contracts Act 1984(opens in new tab)
  • Read the consultation report here(opens in new tab)
  • Provide clarity on the expectations for trustees versus insurers.
  • Include additional information on 'relevant failures' and their application.
  • Expand explanation of trustees' responsibilities under section 52(7)(d) of the Superannuation Industry (Supervision) Act 1993.
  • Clarify the context for seeking evidence of wording applied by other reasonable and prudent insurers.
  • Provide further context on expectations for statutory declarations.
  • Add and clarified case studies to ensure broad understanding.
  • Read the consultation report here(opens in new tab)

Delayed Insurance Claims in Superannuation:

  • The AFCA Approach to delayed insurance claims in superannuation(opens in new tab)
  • Read the consultation report here(opens in new tab)
  • Clarify that determinations address whether the trustee's or insurer's decision was fair and reasonable.
  • Add clarification on how AFCA considers complaints involving circumstances beyond an insurer's control.
  • Explain when trustees should bring claims to the insurer's attention.
  • Update references to relevant documents for information requirements.
  • Provide additional information on consequences for unreasonable delays.
  • Clarify AFCA's rules regarding non-financial loss in superannuation complaints.
  • Expand explanation of AFCA's systemic issues and Code Compliance functions.
  • Clarify and added case studies to illustrate the application of the Approach.
  • Read the consultation report here(opens in new tab)
