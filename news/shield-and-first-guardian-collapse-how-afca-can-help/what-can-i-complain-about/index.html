Who can I complain about?

These matters can be complex. You may have interacted with multiple entities, including financial advice firms (licensees), financial advisers, brokers or authorised representatives, superannuation trustees, or responsible entities of investment schemes (such as a Managed Investment Scheme).

It’s important to lodge your complaint about any financial firm involved in your investment journey that you have concerns about. More than one financial firm may have done something that contributed to your loss. AFCA cannot tell you who to complain about, but you can submit complaints about more than one firm at the same time. If you want to complain about more than one firm, you should submit a separate complaint for each firm and each complaint should mention the other complaints you are submitting.

A typical investment journey

It is open to you to lodge a complaint about any party that has been involved in your investment journey, that you have concerns about. A typical investment journey might involve how you were introduced to the product, anyone you relied on to invest (such as a licensee and its authorised advisors or brokers), where, how and who you made the investment with.

Once you have made a complaint or multiple complaints, AFCA will assess whether we can consider any or all of your complaints.

Here is how to work it out:

Complaints about financial advice

Under AFCA’s Rules, a Financial Firm includes any representatives of that firm. This means that if you received financial advice from an individual adviser or advice company, your complaint should usually be lodged against the licensee (the firm that authorised the adviser to provide advice).

If you’re unsure who the relevant AFCA member is, you can look at your advice documents and use the ASIC Professional Registers to find out:

If you search for a financial advice firm:

You can also check any advice documents you received to identify who provided you with advice.

Please note: Some advisers or firms may have been authorised by more than one licensee. If that’s the case, you may need to submit a complaint for each issue to ensure AFCA can consider all relevant advice.

  • Authorised representatives (e.g. individual advisers) are sometimes but not generally AFCA members.
  • Complaints about their conduct are therefore usually made about the responsible licensee (the AFCA member firm).
  • Visit the ASIC register.
  • Enter the name of the adviser or advice company.
  • Click on the name/profile of the representative
  • Check the financial firm name under ‘Licensees represented’
  • Click on the name/profile of the financial advice firm
  • Click the drop down listed titled “representatives” for an alphabetical list to find the advisor/representative that gave you the advice.

Complaints about Managed Investment Schemes (MISs) and their Responsible Entities (REs)

AFCA has a limited jurisdiction in relation to complaints about Managed Investment Schemes (MIS). We cannot consider complaints about the management of the fund or scheme as a whole. We can consider other complaints brought against the Responsible Entities of a MIS, for example about misleading conduct.

In some circumstances, the Responsible Entity is also licensed to provide personal financial advice to retail clients and may have done so in some instances.

Complaints involving SMSFs and Superannuation Trustees

Sometimes these matters involve investments made through a Self-Managed Superannuation Fund (SMSF). Sometimes the investment has instead been made through a superannuation wrap product with a superannuation trustee. In these cases:

You can also contact your superannuation trustee directly for more information about your investment. They may be able to clarify the structure of your fund and help you understand your options.

AFCA has a limited jurisdiction in relation to complaints about superannuation funds. We cannot consider complaints about the management of the fund or scheme as a whole. We can consider other complaints brought against superannuation trustees, for example about misleading conduct or financial advice a trustee representative has provided.

If you have any concerns about the conduct of a superannuation trustee, it is open to you to submit a complaint about that to AFCA.

  • AFCA’s Factsheet about SMSF complaints here
  • If you received advice to set up or invest through an SMSF and you are concerned about that advice, it is open to you to submit your complaint about the advice firm or licensee that provided that advice.
  • If you have concerns about the superannuation trustee or the investment platform provider managing your superannuation, it is open to you to submit a separate complaint with the relevant trustee.
