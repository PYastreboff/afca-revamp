Supporting Buy Now Pay Later reforms (2025)

AFCA welcomes enhanced protections for Australian consumers using Buy Now Pay Later (BNPL) products, including low cost credit contracts.

AFCA welcomes enhanced protections for Australian consumers using Buy Now Pay Later (BNPL) products, including low cost credit contracts.

Australian consumers using Buy Now Pay Later (BNPL) products, including low cost credit contracts, now have access to free, fair and independent dispute resolution through AFCA when they are unable to resolve complaints directly with their provider. This follows legislation that extends application of the National Credit Act and National Credit Code to Buy Now Pay Later arrangements - and introduces low cost credit contracts as a new category of regulated credit in Australia.

Under the changes, which take effect from 10 June 2025:

AFCA is committed to ensuring a smooth implementation of these changes for consumers, financial firms, and our expert teams who support them.

  • All providers of Buy Now Pay Later products, including low cost credit contracts, must comply with the National Credit Act, National Credit Code, and responsible lending obligations. They must also consider financial hardship requests and credit reporting.
  • Mandatory obligations for providers will include holding an Australian Credit Licence, becoming a member of AFCA if not already, and establishing appropriate processes and mechanisms for addressing consumer complaints.
  • Full detail on the new obligations is available via the Australian Securities & Investments Commission (ASIC) website: Buy Now Pay Later | ASIC

For consumers

  • Australians experiencing issues with a Buy Now Pay Later product must first try to resolve their complaint directly with their provider.
  • From 10 June 2025, Australians who use Buy Now Pay Later products (including low cost credit contracts) will have access to free, fair, and independent dispute resolution through AFCA if they are unable to resolve complaints directly with their provider. All BNPL providers in Australia must be members of AFCA by this date.
  • Use AFCA's financial firm search function to find your BNPL provider, contact us directly via our Live Webchat or call us on 1800 931 678 .
  • Please note: If a Buy Now Pay Later provider is not yet a member of AFCA, we cannot consider complaints against them.

For BNPL providers

  • Following updated regulatory guidance from ASIC, providers of Buy Now Pay Later products and other Low-Cost Credit Contracts must be AFCA members, hold an Australian Credit Licence, and meet mandatory obligations to operate legally in Australia from 10 June 2025.
  • We recommend that any new AFCA membership applications are submitted by 26 May 2025 to ensure AFCA has sufficient time to process the application before regulatory changes take effect on 10 June.
  • Please note: A firm must obtain AFCA membership before applying for an Australian Credit Licence through ASIC. Please factor this sequence (and ASIC processing times) when planning applications.

What to know about these reforms

The aim of the Buy Now Pay Later reforms (effective as of 10 June 2025) is to protect consumers in a way that is flexible, adaptable, and proportionate to the risk of consumer harm. While we don’t set regulatory guidance, AFCA is committed to ensuring a smooth implementation of these reforms - for consumers, financial firms, and our expert teams who support them. We appreciate new and existing member firms that provide Buy Now Pay Later products and Low-Cost Credit products may be unsure of AFCA’s role as a dispute resolution provider in applying the reforms to complaints from 10 June.

AFCA is here to help

  • AFCA continues to engage closely with industry and ASIC to support implementation of the changes. We will continue to share the latest information and supporting links on this website.
  • If you need to apply for AFCA membership, visit our support page: Apply for AFCA membership
  • If you are a new member, or unfamiliar with AFCA, our dedicated Member Resources will help you understand and navigate AFCA’s complaint resolution process, jurisdiction and approach.
  • It is important to note AFCA’s Fee Structure and how fees and charges work for the current period and new financial year beginning 1 July 2025.

Key dates

  • 26 May 2025 – Recommended final day for AFCA membership applications to be received ensuring these new applications are processed ahead of the regulatory deadline.
  • 10 June 2025 – New regulatory requirements take effect.
