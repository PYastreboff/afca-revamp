Membership

Our current annual registration fee is published on this page.

We process membership applications every Friday. Applications received prior to 8am Friday will be processed and approved that week. Applications received after 8am Friday will be processed and approved the following week.

Once your membership has been accepted, we will send you an email to confirm and provide you with a membership number.

The easiest way to update your contact details is via the member portal. Registered member contacts can log in to the member portal at any time, from any location and update their contact details.

Authorised member contacts can ask to cancel their membership by submitting a request in the member portal.

Name changes can be made by submitting a request in the member portal. Please attach an ASIC Name Change Certificate so we can verify the change.

AFCA will no longer issue membership certificates from FY26. This means members will not receive a membership certificate upon payment of their annual registration fee. We have shared more about this here.

If members believe a complaint is lodged against the wrong financial firm, they can use the portal to inform AFCA. AFCA staff will review the request and consider the most appropriate action.

In most cases, we will discuss this with the complainant. If they agree, we will update the complaint with the correct financial firm’s details.

If the complainant disagrees, the financial firm can raise a Jurisdictional Review directly via the member portal. The matter will then be considered by AFCA.

Learn more about this process here.

Cancelling membership

If a cancellation request is not received by this date, the renewal fee will be charged and is payable.

The 12-month notice period allows customers to lodge a complaint in relation to prior financial services provided.

At the end of this 12-month period, your membership will automatically cancel. You do not need to take any further action.

Please note members are to finalise any outstanding fees on their account.

Members will be required to handle any complaints lodged within the 12-month notice period.

Applicable complaint handling fees will apply.

Rule A.11.6 – publishing names of financial firms that fail to comply with AFCA Determinations

AFCA is required to notify the relevant regulators wLearn more a financial firm has not given effect to a determination. AFCA may also publish information under Rule A.11.6, which is considered separately.

AFCA does not routinely monitor compliance after a determination is accepted. Concerns about implementation may be raised by a complainant or identified through other interactions with the firm.

WLearn more a concern is raised, AFCA will make enquiries with the financial firm to understand the circumstances and determine whether the determination has been complied with.

We will contact the firm to understand the circumstances and whether the determination has been complied with.

If a Financial Firm does not meet the requirements of an AFCA Determination, Rule A.11.6 enables us to publish the name of the firm on our website and, for example, in a media release. This promotes transparency, accountability and fairness to other Financial Firms and consumers.

The relevant Rule permitting publication on AFCA’s website operates as a discretion, and AFCA decides whether publication is appropriate in the particular circumstances of the case.

AFCA does not publish complainant details, reasons for non-compliance, or findings of misconduct. Publication identifies a failure to give effect to a determination and does not of itself indicate a breach of law or other regulatory action.

A determination must be implemented by the date specified in the determination. If it is not, AFCA may form the view that the determination has not been given effect to. AFCA will contact the financial firm to understand the circumstances and the steps being taken to give effect to the determination. We encourage firms to engage with AFCA as early as possible if tLearn more are difficulties implementing a determination.

Whether publication is appropriate will depend on the particular circumstances, including the firm’s engagement and the reason for the delay.

The information published reflects the position at the date of publication. AFCA does not update the report if a financial firm later complies with the determination. The report will include a disclaimer explaining this.

Sharing feedback

We encourage and value member input on matters such as changes to our Rules, submissions to our Independent Reviews and feedback on our approach to complaints. Also, all members have a vote at annual general meetings.

If you lodge a complaint about our service, we will investigate and consider the concerns you have raised and respond to them.

If your complaint requires further action, we will work with you to find an appropriate solution.

Complaints about our service must be lodged within six months of the related financial firm complaint being closed.
