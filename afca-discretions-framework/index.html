AFCA Discretions Framework

AFCA is committed to resolving complaints in a fair, independent, and transparent manner. To achieve this, AFCA may exercise specific discretionary powers as outlined in the AFCA Rules (the Rules).

This discretionary authority is guided by our scheme principles, which ensure that all decisions are made fairly, consistently and transparently. Although AFCA is not subject to the Administrative Decisions (Judicial Review) Act, its decision-making under the Rules is informed by the scheme principles, drawing on key administrative law principles to promote fairness and transparency. By aligning our discretionary practices with these foundational principles, we uphold our commitment to delivering fair and impartial outcomes for all parties involved.

Understanding AFCA’s Discretions Framework

At AFCA, our approach to managing the exercise of discretion under our Rules is grounded in a commitment to fairness, consistency, and accountability.

Under AFCA’s Discretions Framework, we have established clear principles that guide how discretion is applied, ensuring that decisions are made thoughtfully and in alignment with our overarching goal of fair, independent and effective solutions for financial disputes.

We support the application of these principles through comprehensive policies and internal guidance, so that discretion can be exercised consistently and effectively across all cases. We also delegate authority to exercise various discretionary powers, including mechanisms for reviewing those decisions to maintain integrity and trust.

Additionally, our governance framework continuously monitors and refines how discretion is applied, ensuring that our processes remain procedurally fair, transparent and responsive to the needs of all parties involved.

Principles Guiding the Exercise of Discretion

As part of AFCA’s Discretions Framework, the following principles serve as the foundation for how we exercise decision-making under our Rules, reflecting the relevant scheme principles and ensuring that our approach is fair, consistent, and transparent for all parties involved.

Below, we outline the principles at a high level, and provide commentary on how each is applied in our approach:

Accessible (AFCA Rule A.2.1b)

AFCA is dedicated to providing an accessible complaint resolution service. When exercising discretionary powers, AFCA takes care not to exclude complaints or individuals from the scheme without good reason. The focus is on ensuring that all complainants, particularly those who are vulnerable or need extra assistance, have fair access to AFCA’s services, particularly in circumstances where a relevant alternate forum may not be available. This principle emphasises the importance of allowing individuals to bring their disputes to AFCA for resolution and ensures that discretionary decisions do not unduly limit access.

Independent and Impartial (AFCA Rule A.2.1c)(j))

Independence and impartiality are central to AFCA’s operations. Decisions are made without bias or undue influence from either party involved in a complaint. Those exercising discretion must be free of any conflicts of interest to ensure that decisions are fair and unbiased. This principle ensures that every complaint is considered on its merits and that both parties are treated equally and fairly.

Procedural Fairness (AFCA Rule A.2.1c)(i))

AFCA is committed to ensuring procedural fairness in the handling of complaints. This means that before exercising some discretionary powers, AFCA will generally hear from all parties involved to understand their position and provide an opportunity to be heard. Procedural fairness is particularly important when a discretionary decision resolves a complaint. However, in some cases, such as setting procedural timelines or choosing the resolution method, AFCA may not consult the parties first. Even in these cases, AFCA applies its experience to ensure fairness in all actions.

Fair (AFCA Rule A.2.1c)(i))

AFCA seeks to achieve outcomes that are substantively fair. This involves considering all relevant information, the interests, and the specific circumstances of the parties involved, including any vulnerabilities they may be experiencing. When exercising discretion, AFCA aims to apply the Rules and operational guidelines in a way that is fair, balanced and considered, ensuring that each decision reflects the intent of the Rules and the principles underlying the scheme.

Efficiency, Effectiveness and Timeliness (AFCA Rule A.2.1(c)(iii))

AFCA’s goal is to resolve complaints efficiently, effectively, and in a timely manner. In exercising discretion, AFCA strives to facilitate timely resolution without compromising fairness. Decisions to exclude complaints, for example those lacking merit, are made as soon as it is appropriate to do so, ensuring that all parties are informed promptly and that unnecessary delays are avoided.

Cooperative with Minimum of Formality (AFCA Rule A.2.1(c)(iv))

AFCA aims to handle complaints cooperatively and with minimal formality. This means working closely with the parties involved to gather all necessary information and understand the key issues. The approach is designed to be user-friendly, particularly for those who may be unfamiliar with AFCA’s processes or who are not represented.

Consistency in Decision-Making (AFCA Rule A.2.1(d))

Consistency is vital to AFCA’s decision-making process. While each complaint is unique, AFCA ensures that discretionary powers are exercised in a way that treats similar complaints similarly. This promotes fairness and predictability, providing confidence to all parties that like cases will be handled in a like manner.

Appropriate Expertise and Resources (AFCA Rule A.2.1(e))

AFCA ensures that those exercising discretionary powers have the appropriate expertise and experience. Decisions are made by staff who are knowledgeable and skilled, with more complex or sensitive discretionary decisions being handled by senior staff. This principle guarantees that the use of discretion is informed, appropriate, and aligned with AFCA’s standards.

Transparency (AFCA Rule A.2.1f))

Transparency is key to maintaining trust in AFCA’s processes. When exercising discretionary powers, especially in cases where a complaint is excluded, AFCA provides clear reasons for its decisions. This openness helps all parties understand the basis for decisions and fosters a sense of fairness and accountability.

Commitment to Continuous Improvement (AFCA Rule A.2.1(j))

AFCA is committed to continuously improving the way it exercises its discretionary powers. Through regular supervision, peer review, and quality assurance processes, AFCA seeks to learn from complaint-handling and refine its decision-making practices. This ongoing commitment means AFCA’s use of discretion remains fair, effective, and aligned with its guiding principles.

AFCA’s Guide to Exercising Discretion

Ensure Accessibility

Does the exercise of discretion unnecessarily restrict access to AFCA's services, especially for individuals who may need extra help or are experiencing vulnerable circumstances?

Maintain Independence and Impartiality

Ensure procedural fairness

Have all parties have a chance to present their views before a decision is made? What is appropriate to close?

For procedural decisions, such as setting deadlines or selecting the dispute resolution method, AFCA may exercise discretion without seeking prior input from the parties. These decisions should be informed by AFCA’s expertise and experience in determining what is fair and effective.

Act Fairly

Promote Efficiency, Effectiveness and Timeliness

Has the discretion been exercised in a manner that facilitates the prompt and effective resolution of the complaint? Decisions to exclude complaints or expedite processes should be made as early as possible when appropriate, ensuring timely outcomes for all parties.

Encourage Co-operation and Minimise Formality

Ensure Consistency

Maintain Transparency

Apply Appropriate Expertise and Resources

Has discretion been exercised by individuals with the necessary expertise and experience? This may involve consulting with senior personnel or experts within AFCA to make well-informed decisions, particularly in complex or sensitive cases.

Foster Continuous Improvement

Utilise feedback, peer review, and quality assurance processes to continually improve how discretionary powers are exercised. Regularly review decisions to ensure they align with AFCA’s guiding principles and improve upon them where necessary.
