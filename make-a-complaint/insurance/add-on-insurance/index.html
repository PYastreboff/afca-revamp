Add-on Insurance

For complaints involving add-on insurance sold before July 2019

For complaints involving add-on insurance sold before July 2019

The deadline for lodging complaints regarding an add-on insurance product sold before July 2019 has now passed (unless special circumstances apply).

Complaints may still be lodged. However, in most cases we are likely to find the complaint falls outside our Rules. Submissions should reflect this and clearly explain why the complaint may still be within scope.

Note: the information below is not intended to cover general service or insurance claim complaints relating to add-on insurance.

For more information regarding what we expect from parties lodging complaints from 1 July 2025 regarding add-on insurance products sold before July 2019, click Learn more.

What is Add-On Insurance?

Add-on insurance is a type of insurance that is often sold alongside other products, like loans, credit cards, or car finance.

Add-on insurance policies can include things like:

Financial firms may offer add-on insurance to consumers along with car loans, novated leases, personal loans, home loans and credit / store cards.

Add-on insurance may be a stand-alone policy paid upfront, paid monthly or financed into a line of credit.

  • Consumer Credit Insurance (CCI)
  • Guaranteed Asset Protection Insurance (GAP Insurance)
  • Tyre and Rim Insurance
  • Extended Warranty / Mechanical Breakdown Insurance.

Complaints AFCA sees regarding the sale of add-on insurance

AFCA has a dedicated fact sheet about common issues regarding the sale of add-on insurance and how we handle these complaints. You can access the fact sheet Learn more.

Timeframe for lodging your complaint

As part of AFCA’s non-superannuation complaints jurisdiction, we can generally only consider a complaint if it is made within six years since the complainant first became aware, or ‘should reasonably have become aware’, that they suffered the loss they want to complain about.

The sale of add-on insurance and its value has been the subject of significant media, regulator, industry and consumer advocacy campaigns and class actions for many years. These products were also the subject of recommendations by the Financial Services Royal Commission in its final report published on 4 February 2019 and subsequent media attention.

The right to lodge a complaint at AFCA however is not open ended. Aged complaints lodged many years after the events complained about, result in difficulties for everyone in obtaining and assessing relevant information, documents, and recollections.

Consequently, unless the complainant can show special circumstances, we will likely find a complaint about the sale of add-on insurance sold before July 2019, lodged at AFCA after 30 June 2025, falls outside our Rules.

It is for AFCA to apply its jurisdiction to each case on its merits. AFCA’s expectation is that Financial Firms should still engage and resolve add-on insurance complaints as if AFCA’s jurisdiction applies, and consistent with their internal dispute obligations.

How to make a complaint about add-on insurance policies sold after July 2019

AFCA’s usual time limits under our Rules will apply. If you wish to make a complaint, it is best to act now and without delay.

This information is designed for consumers who wish to make a complaint about add-on insurance. Information for Paid Representatives is noted separately below.

You can check if you’ve been paying for add-on insurance by reviewing your loan paperwork and bank statements for any insurance-related payments.

You can check if you’ve received any letters from your bank or lender about remediation programs – many financial institutions have offered or are offering refunds for mis-sold add-on insurance that provided little to no value. If you’ve been offered a refund and you’re happy then you don’t need to take any further action.

If you haven’t received a remediation notice or you’re not happy with the offer, you can still make a complaint if:

  • You didn’t realise you were paying for add-on insurance with your credit card or loan
  • You thought the insurance was compulsory when it was optional
  • You were sold insurance you couldn’t claim on (for example, if you didn’t meet the employment conditions like minimum working hours).

How to lodge a complaint

Before proceeding, gather all your supporting documentation, including policy documents and account statements, or any other relevant information to support your case. If you don’t have these documents, contact your financial firm.

To lodge a complaint about add-on insurance, you should first talk to your financial firm (bank, lender or insurer) directly. They then have 30 days to resolve your complaint with you directly.

If the matter is unresolved after this period, you can escalate it to AFCA online (Learn more) or by calling 1800 931 678.

You do not need to be represented

AFCA is a free service to Australian consumers. Our processes are designed so that you can bring a complaint to AFCA without the need for assistance.

Because it is not a requirement to use a paid representative to use AFCA’s service, the fee/s paid to the representative will not be awarded as part of any compensation provided in the outcome of a complaint.

What to expect from AFCA and your financial firm

A dispute resolution specialist will work with you and your financial firm to find a solution. AFCA will help negotiate an agreement between all parties. If no agreement can be reached, an ombudsman will investigate the complaint and make a final decision.

AFCA will work with both parties to help negotiate an agreement and if no agreement can be reached, we will provide a view on the merits of the complaint. If we make a determination that is in the consumer’s favour and they accept it, the financial firm is required to comply with the determination and any remedy that we award.

When determining a complaint, AFCA must do what is fair in all the circumstances, and take into account legal principles, good industry practice, applicable industry codes of conduct, and any previous relevant determinations made (although we are not bound by these prior determinations).

Complaints lodged on or after 1 July 2025

Reasonable awareness of loss for add-on insurance complaints

AFCA’s Fact Sheet for add-on insurance outlines AFCA’s approach to reasonable awareness of loss for add-on insurance complaints. All parties should read and be familiar with it. You can access the fact sheet Learn more.

AFCA will generally only consider complaints regarding the sale of add-on insurance sold before July 2019 if they were submitted to AFCA by 30 June 2025.

For complaints lodged after 30 June 2025, AFCA may assess the complaint as outside AFCA’s usual six-year time limit if a consumer purchased add-on insurance prior to July 2019, unless the complainant can demonstrate that special circumstances apply. We will assess this on a case-by-case basis.

The below information provides guidance to parties for complaints about policies sold before July 2019 and lodged at AFCA after 30 June 2025.

If AFCA finds the complaint is not within jurisdiction (including as to time limits), the complaint will close.

Guidance for consumers

When lodging a complaint, you should outline:

We decide if special circumstances apply case by case. When assessing whether special circumstances apply, we consider factors such as those below.

More information about our usual time limits and when we may extend them, including as to special circumstances, is outlined on pages 117 to 120 in our Operational Guidelines (dated 1 July 2024). You can access the Operational Guidelines Learn more.

  • when you first became aware of your loss; and
  • whether any special circumstances apply which means you did not lodge the complaint before 30 June 2025, and, if so, information in support of this.
  • The reason for the delay: You need to show that something unusual and outside your control prevented you from submitting the complaint on time. Just saying you were not aware of AFCA is not enough.
  • How much you would be disadvantaged: If not extending the deadline would cause you serious disadvantage, we will consider that. But merely the deadline expiring does not automatically count as special.
  • How long the delay was: The longer you waited to complain after the deadline, the harder it is for us to agree to an extension.
  • The level of prejudice to the financial firm: If a lot of time has passed between the event and the complaint being lodged, the financial firm may no longer reasonably have records to defend its position. If this happens, we may consider that the delay has put the financial firm at a disadvantage.

Additional guidance for financial firms

AFCA’s expectation is that financial firms will engage and resolve add-on insurance complaints regardless of AFCA’s jurisdictional limits, and consistent with their internal dispute resolution obligations. If AFCA becomes aware of conduct inconsistent with those obligations, we may investigate further and/or engage with the relevant regulator(s), in accordance with our obligations.

If a financial firm considers the complaint is outside AFCA’s jurisdiction, please provide a submission addressing the jurisdictional issues. Ensure you:

Regardless of whether jurisdiction is disputed, we expect financial firms to comply with the add-on insurance EDR response guide. This ensures AFCA can deal with complaints in a timely and efficient manner if we find the complaint is within jurisdiction. The response guide can be found Learn more.

  • outline, then address, each ‘claim’ for loss. This is because AFCA will consider jurisdiction for each separate claim;
  • refer to the specific AFCA Rule/s to ensure that AFCA and the complainant understand the jurisdictional issue/s being raised;
  • refer to the AFCA Operational Guidelines and any relevant approach documents; and
  • if the complainant or their representative has raised special circumstances, to the extent you disagree, provide a submission addressing why.
