A service we can be proud of, and a future we are ready for

In 2024-25, AFCA continued to deliver on its purpose, providing fair, timely and independent resolution of financial complaints, while preparing for the future. We received 100,745 complaints and resolved more than 101,000; a strong result that delivered over $390 million in compensation and refunds to consumers and small business owners. Most complaints were resolved by agreement, but where needed, we issued more than 4,780 determinations to provide finality.

Complaint trends and sector challenges

General insurance complaints rose 17%, driven by a surge in add-on insurance complaints. Investments and advice complaints increased 18%, due largely to firm collapses, while life insurance complaints rose 5%. Superannuation complaints fell 16%, including a 39% drop in claims delay complaints, a positive shift, but this is from a record high, and service-related issues remain a concern.

Banking and finance complaints declined 9%, including a 45% drop in scam-related complaints. While this is encouraging, the overall number of scam complaints remains too high. Behind each one is someone who may have lost life-changing amounts of money, and the trauma caused by scams is immense. The passage of the Scams Prevention Framework is a critical step forward.

Laying strong foundations

We have now embedded a major IT transformation, following the launch of a new case management system and member and consumer portals. These systems handled over 66,000 online complaints and supported the onboarding of 7,731 members. While the rollout presented challenges, we are already seeing improvements in efficiency and service.

Using our voice to influence and improve

AFCA’s role goes beyond resolving individual complaints. In 2024-25, we made 21 submissions to Government, regulators and industry consultations on issues ranging from credit reporting, financial abuse to scams and insurance reform. We also consulted on seven AFCA Approach documents and published five new or updated versions, helping parties to understand how we make decisions, which support prompt and more consistent resolutions.

Our systemic issues work continues to drive meaningful change. We identified and resolved issues affecting more than 527,000 consumers, resulting in over $7.3 million in refunds. AFCA reported 105 systemic issues to regulators, including three serious contraventions of the law.

Our people

Our refreshed Employee Value Proposition, Fairness Feels Good, was launched during 2024-25, and we continue to invest in wellbeing, capability and inclusion. We also introduced new family violence policies, expanded cultural safety training, and continued our reconciliation journey, guided by the concept of Ngalimba: loving energy and finding strength to help others.

Looking ahead

Our Strategic Plan 2025-27, Towards an Australia free from financial disputes, sets a clear direction. We will continue to strengthen our core service, support earlier resolution, and contribute to broader system improvements.

Thank you to our Independent Chair John Pollaers OAM and the Board, for their support, wise counsel and diligent governance of AFCA. Thank you to AFCA’s staff for the difficult job that they do. Working on complaints with parties in dispute every day takes great skill, empathy and resilience. Thank you also to all stakeholders for your ongoing partnership and trust, as AFCA continues to work to deliver fair outcomes for often complex matters

David Locke Chief Executive Officer and Chief Ombudsman
