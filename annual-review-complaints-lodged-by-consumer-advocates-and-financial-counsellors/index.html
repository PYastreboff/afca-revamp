Key complaint trends

In 2024-25, AFCA referrals from consumer advocates rose sharply, increasing from 4,166 to 6,571. All advocacy groups made more referrals, but growth was largest from financial counsellors (up 70%) and community lawyers (up 89%).

AFCA understands the link between financial stress and overall wellbeing. We are working to make our complaint process fair, efficient and accessible for everyone.

Consumer advocates, including financial counsellors and community lawyers offer critical support, especially for individuals with complex needs. Almost one in three people they represented in 2024‑25 was experiencing difficult circumstances, such as family violence, financial abuse, a mental health condition, housing insecurity or chronic illness.

In 2024‑25, consumer advocates referred 6,571 people to AFCA – a significant increase from 2023‑24. They lodged 715 complaints directly. Nearly three quarters were from financial counsellors.

As cost‑of‑living pressures continue their impact, AFCA expects to see more complaints about financial difficulty and affordability. We will keep working closely with consumer advocates to support people under pressure.

Industry trends and challenges

The number of complaints lodged directly by advocates fell slightly from 754 in 2023‑24 to 715 in 2024‑25. Advocates could be referring people to AFCA rather than representing them, due to resourcing or funding constraints. For example, financial counsellors are funded to assist with credit and debt matters, but not insurance claims – except in cases of a natural disaster. There is a significant gap in support for people facing barriers in the insurance claims process.

Personal loans, home loans and credit cards were again the most common products in advocate‑lodged complaints, with most cases linked to financial firms’ decisions and financial difficulty.

Geographically, complaints lodged by financial counsellors were most concentrated in Victoria (47%) and New South Wales (17%). Increases were seen in the ACT, South Australia and Northern Territory, which could reflect improved outreach or rising financial stress in those areas.
