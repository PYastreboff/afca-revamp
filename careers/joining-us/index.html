Our Hiring Process

AFCA's recruitment process is designed to be inclusive and fair, provide the best possible experience for all stakeholders, and ensure that AFCA has a diverse workforce comprising the most talented people solving our biggest challenges.

We encourage applications from people of all ages, cultures, abilities, sexual orientations and gender identities. We strive to provide a recruitment experience that's fair, inclusive and accessible. If you have any specific accessibility requirements, please reach out to the recruiter who is managing the process or email us at Email careers(opens in new tab).

Using AI during the recruitment process

We get it, we love what AI can do as well. We also know, like everything tLearn more(opens in new tab) is a time and place for it’s use. So, to ensure Fairness Feels Good during your recruitment process, we thought we’d share upfront how and when you can use AI while applying for roles at AFCA.

- Feel free to use AI to help translate your experience, skills and achievements into a resume or cover letter (but please still proof read and ensure it is accurate, AI has a habit of adding experience that you don’t have)

- Use AI to learn more about AFCA, our values, the impact me make and how Fairness would feel for you

- Exploring industry or professional trends to relate your experiences to

- Use AI to prepare for interviews with practise questions, help draw out relevant and real examples you may want to highlight at interview.

- Please don’t use AI for formal assessments throughout our process such as case studies or cognitive assessment or personality profiles

- Don’t use AI during phone screens or formal interviews (if you require assistance to perform at your best, let us know and we will happily discuss)

- Don’t have your referees use AI when providing formal references.

We know this is emerging technology and we want you to feel confident and perform at your best. So, if in doubt ask our team and we’ll happily guide you.
