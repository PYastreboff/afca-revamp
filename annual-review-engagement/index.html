Who we engage with

We work with service users, people working toward industry reform and many in‑between:

Stakeholder feedback plays a crucial role in helping us to improve services. We seek it via:

  • We actively collaborate with financial firms, industry and Government to share insights, help lift industry standards and reduce complaints.
  • We regularly interact with consumer advocates, such as financial counsellors, community lawyers and financial capability workers.
  • We regularly engage with other ombudsman schemes to identify intersectionality and share best practice learnings.
  • forums and liaison groups
  • one‑on‑one meetings and consultations
  • industry events and webinars
  • newsletters and social media.

Member engagement

We work closely with members to improve complaint handling, support better industry practices and help to prevent disputes. We invite members to consultations, webinars and forums.

During 2024‑25, we met regularly with boards and executives of major banks, insurers and other bodies to share insights on scams, delays, rising complaint volumes and more.

Member services

AFCA’s dedicated membership team helps firms to manage their membership. They look after:

In 2024‑25, AFCA elevated the Customer Experience function through targeted, people focused capability building and strategic alignment. Our focus on understanding and enhancing end‑to‑end journeys for both members and consumers ensures needs, expectations and feedback are at the heart of how we design and deliver services.

  • new member applications
  • changes to existing memberships
  • the member portal
  • everyday enquiries
  • membership renewals and expulsions
  • reporting to the regulator on ‘at risk’ members.

Member forums

AFCA held Member Forums in October 2024 and March 2025, featuring tailored sessions across key areas including banking and finance, small business, superannuation, life insurance, general insurance, and investments and advice. Collectively, nearly 9,000 members attended these forums, reflecting strong engagement across the sector.

To further strengthen our connection with members and create more engaging conversations, we reviewed and refreshed the forum format, addressing key topics and themes across each product area. The new approach has been well received, with strong participation and positive feedback, which reflects the value of more interactive and relevant discussions.

Member news

Our member news provides regular updates on AFCA’s dispute resolution and developments across the broader financial services industry. It is shared monthly with our entire member base.

To support continuous improvement and transparency, we also publish a dedicated bulletin on our member portal. It tells members and other users about enhancements, fixes and updates to the AFCA portal.

Engagement with consumers

Our Community Engagement team keeps AFCA connected to a wide range of voices, helping build trust and improve access to our services. They lead our outreach program and consumer engagement work – gathering insights, keeping us informed about emerging issues, and refining how we support consumers.

AFCA Consumer Advisory Panel

AFCA’s 11‑member Consumer Advisory Panel (ACAP) met our Senior Leadership Group every quarter.

The ACAP provided valuable insights into AFCA’s strategy, policy development and consumer‑focused initiatives. Members shared real‑time perspectives on the financial challenges impacting Australians, helping to shape our understanding and response. Key topics included a deeper exploration of AFCA’s jurisdiction, rules and policies – particularly the upcoming Scams Prevention Framework and the consultation schedule for Approaches.

Panel meetings are not just a platform for AFCA to share complaint trends and strategic updates. We like to hear directly from ACAP’s members. This ensures our work stays grounded in both lived experience and sector expertise, which strengthens our commitment to fair, transparent and responsive service delivery.

Consumer Advocate Liaison Meetings

Each quarter, our senior team meets with representatives from nearly 30 services, spanning advocacy, financial counselling and community legal. These Consumer Advocate Liaison Meetings (CALM) focus on:

AFCA gains insight into managing challenging behaviour and supporting victim‑survivors of domestic violence. We are deeply grateful for the time, wisdom and dedication of everyone involved.

  • improving access to EDR
  • supporting vulnerable customers
  • examining fairness, scams and systemic issues.

Outreach

Face‑to‑face interactions are the gold standard for engagement, building trust and deepening connections within the communities we serve. In 2024‑25, we participated in 42 outreach events, focusing on connecting with First Nations peoples, people experiencing domestic violence, LGBTQ+ individuals and remote Australians. The events provided opportunities for direct dialogue, tailored support and strengthened relationships with those who may face barriers to accessing services.

Engagement with Government

Throughout the year, we worked closely with the Federal Government to provide insights into a wide range of issues within the financial sector. By sharing complaints data and trends with ministers, parliamentarians and Government departments, we helped to highlight key problems, affecting both consumers and firms.

AFCA appeared before three parliamentary inquiries covering topics, such as superannuation and Australia’s retirement system, insurance and the affordability of premiums, financial abuse and wealth management companies. Most notably, we engaged closely with MPs and Senators as the Scams Prevention Framework Bill successfully made its way through Parliament. Our involvement included a submission to the Senate Economics Legislation Committee’s inquiry into the bill, appearing before it at a public hearing.

We also assisted the electorate offices of parliamentarians with over 100 requests, either advocating for, or helping constituents with complaints against financial firms.

Submissions

Policy work supports AFCA’s strategic goal of Influence, using quantitative data and qualitative insights to drive improvements across the financial services sector. Through public submissions, we help to shape policy and regulatory settings to improve financial firm behaviour, reduce harm to consumers and small businesses, and minimise the likelihood of future complaints.

In 2024‑25, we made 21 submissions informing Government, parliament, regulatory and industry code consultation processes on topics as diverse as:

  • Centrepay reforms
  • credit reporting framework review
  • wealth management companies
  • financial abuse
  • unfair trading practices
  • reforms in banking
  • general and life insurance
  • scams prevention framework.

International engagement

Connecting with international ombudsman schemes and industry member bodies allows us to share ideas and benchmark best practice, adding to the global conversation. It also helps to strengthen dispute resolution in Australia.

We are an active member of the International Network of Financial Services Ombudsman Schemes, and our Chief, Deputy Chief and Lead Ombudsmen are ongoing members of the Australian and New Zealand Ombudsman Association.

Highlights of our international engagement in 2024‑25 included meeting with:

  • delegates from the Banking, Finance and Insurance Institute of Nepal
  • the Australia‑Indonesia Partnership for Economic Development (Prospera)
  • the Financial Services Authority, Indonesia (Otoritas Jasa Keuangan)
  • the Monetary Authority of Singapore
  • the Bank of Thailand
  • the NZ Banking Ombudsman
  • the UK Financial Ombudsman Service
  • the Financial Industry Disputes Resolution Centre in Singapore.

Website

Visit AFCA website presents information about AFCA, what we do, the complaints we hear, updates and the complaint lodging process.

For the first time during 2024‑25, our website received over a million unique visitors, which was an 18% increase from 2023‑24. This shows that many more people are turning to us for help, guidance and support, and that they can find us when they need to.

From 1 July 2024 to 30 June 2025, our website received:

  • 1,007,137 unique visitors
  • 11,598,334 total page views.
